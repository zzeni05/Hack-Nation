# Mammalian Cell Freezing SOP

## Harvest

Harvest adherent mammalian cells at 70-90% confluence. Trypsinize according to the cell-line SOP, neutralize with complete medium, and pellet cells at 200 x g for 5 minutes. Resuspend cells in complete medium and count using the laboratory cell counter or hemocytometer.

Pre-freeze viability must be at least 90% before proceeding. If viability is below 90%, do not freeze the batch without scientist approval.

## Cryoprotectant preparation

The default freezing medium is 10% DMSO and 90% complete growth medium, prepared fresh and chilled to 4C. Mix gently and keep on ice. Avoid exposing cells to DMSO longer than 15 minutes before controlled-rate freezing.

## Equilibration and vialing

Aliquot 1e6 cells per cryovial unless the protocol specifies otherwise. Resuspend each pellet in chilled freezing medium, equilibrate on ice for 10 minutes, and transfer to labeled cryovials.

## Controlled-rate freezing

Freeze cells at approximately -1C per minute to -80C. The preferred equipment is the CryoMed controlled-rate freezer. Mr. Frosty isopropanol containers may be used as backup but must be noted as a deviation because cooling rate is less reproducible.

## Storage and thaw

Transfer cryovials to liquid nitrogen vapor phase storage within 24 hours. For recovery, thaw rapidly in a 37C water bath until a small ice crystal remains, dilute immediately into pre-warmed complete medium, pellet at 200 x g for 5 minutes, and resuspend in fresh medium.
